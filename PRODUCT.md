# Product

## Register

product

## Users

**家属 (Family Members)** — adult children or relatives of elderly community residents. They access the system primarily on mobile or desktop web during work breaks, evenings, or whenever they need to check on their elders. Their core job: monitor health data, pay for services ordered by elderly relatives, receive alerts about emergencies or anomalies, and stay connected to their family's wellbeing.

**老人 (Elders)** — community residents aged 60+, using the system to order services, track health data, participate in activities, and call for emergency assistance.

**管理员 (Admins)** — community staff managing users, services, devices, emergencies, and relationships.

## Product Purpose

智慧社区养老系统 (Smart Community Elderly Care System) connects family members with their elderly relatives through a digital platform. It enables remote health monitoring, service ordering with family proxy payments, emergency alerts, activity participation, and community service discovery. Success means family members feel informed, reassured, and capable of supporting their elders from anywhere.

## Brand Personality

**专业 · 高效 · 现代** (Professional, Efficient, Modern)

The interface should feel like a premium health-tech product — clean, confident, and purposeful. Not clinical or cold, but precise. Not playful or casual, but approachable. The design communicates competence and reliability while remaining human-centered.

Tone: Clear and direct. No unnecessary ornament. Every element justifies its presence.

## Anti-references

Avoid: hospital HIS system aesthetics (cold blue-white, dense tables, clinical sterility). Avoid: consumer social app patterns (playful colors, excessive animation, casual copy). Avoid: generic SaaS templates (overly soft pastels, excessive cards, hero-metric templates).

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration** — Information hierarchy drives layout. Every visual element must serve comprehension.
2. **Efficiency of interaction** — Minimize clicks. Surface the most important data first. Anticipate the family member's immediate question: "Is my family okay?"
3. **Trust through precision** — Data displays must be unambiguous. Numbers are clear, statuses are obvious, alerts are impossible to miss.
4. **Calm technology** — The system should reduce anxiety, not create it. Even alerts should feel managed and actionable, not alarming.
5. **Progressive depth** — Overview first, detail on demand. The dashboard answers "everything okay?" in one glance; drilling down reveals specifics.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Touch targets ≥ 48px for mobile family users
- Text contrast ≥ 4.5:1 for body copy, ≥ 3:1 for large text
- Support for reduced motion preferences
- Font sizing accommodates middle-aged to older family members (many are 40-60+)
